# Menstrual Health Research Findings: Indian Audience Gap Analysis

## Executive Summary

Research reveals critical gaps in menstrual health awareness, management, and support for Indian girls and women. While period tracking apps are growing, they often lack culturally-relevant, health-focused features tailored to Indian needs. The Saheli app has an opportunity to bridge these gaps with evidence-based, Ayurveda-informed wellness recommendations.

---

## Key Health Challenges Identified

### 1. **High Prevalence of Menstrual Disorders**

**Dysmenorrhea (Painful Periods)**
- **Prevalence**: 60-80% of Indian women experience dysmenorrhea
- **Severity**: 62% report severe pain; 24% report very severe pain
- **Impact**: Affects school attendance, work productivity, and quality of life
- **Current Gap**: Limited awareness of pain management strategies beyond medications

**Irregular Periods & Abnormal Bleeding**
- **64% of girls** experience at least one menstrual abnormality
- **87.7% of adolescent girls** suffer from some menstrual problem
- **45% prevalence** of heavy menstrual bleeding
- **Current Gap**: Lack of tracking tools to identify patterns and seek timely medical help

**Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)**
- **Prevalence**: 1 in 5 young Indian women (10-35% depending on diagnostic criteria)
- **Symptoms**: Irregular cycles, heavy bleeding, hormonal imbalances
- **Current Gap**: Low awareness and delayed diagnosis; need for symptom tracking and early detection

### 2. **Mental Health & Psychological Impacts**

**Premenstrual Syndrome (PMS) & PMDD**
- **70% of women** experience premenstrual symptoms (headaches, moodiness, bloating)
- **Severe cases (PMDD)**: Cause depression, anxiety, irritability in luteal phase
- **Current Gap**: Mental health impacts not discussed; no phase-aware emotional support

**Cycle-Related Mental Health Issues**
- Hormonal fluctuations can exacerbate anxiety, depression, and mood disorders
- Stigma prevents women from seeking mental health support
- **Current Gap**: Apps don't connect menstrual cycle to mental wellness

### 3. **Lack of Awareness & Education**

**Myths & Misconceptions**
- Periods still viewed as "impure" or "contaminated" in many communities
- Poor understanding of menstrual physiology and cycle phases
- Unscientific attitudes toward menstrual health management

**Low Health Literacy**
- **Only 30% of women** regularly track their periods (rest rely on memory)
- **91.7% of women** can't access medical services for menstrual health
- **Only 47.6%** of girls with health problems seek medical help
- **Current Gap**: Existing apps don't provide health education or normalize seeking care

### 4. **Period Poverty & Access Issues**

**Socioeconomic Barriers**
- 1 in 5 girls miss school due to lack of menstrual products
- Limited access to hygiene facilities, especially in rural/peri-urban areas
- Stigma prevents open discussion about menstrual needs

**Current Gap**: Apps don't address affordability or provide resource guides

## Ayurvedic & Nutritional Gaps

### Missing Personalized Wellness Support

**Ayurvedic Approach to Menstrual Health**
- Menstrual health reflects balance of Vata, Pitta, and Kapha doshas
- Each cycle phase has specific nutritional and lifestyle needs
- Herbal support varies by dosha imbalance

**Phase-Specific Nutrition Gaps**
- **Menstrual Phase (Days 1-5)**: Iron-rich foods (spinach, lentils, beets), warming spices
- **Follicular Phase (Days 6-13)**: Light, nourishing foods; increased activity
- **Ovulation Phase (Days 14-15)**: Peak energy; antioxidant-rich foods
- **Luteal Phase (Days 16-28)**: Grounding foods, magnesium, reduced stimulation

**Current Gap**: No Indian period tracking apps provide phase-specific, Ayurveda-informed nutrition guidance

### Lifestyle & Wellness Management

**Cycle Syncing Gaps**
- Women unaware of how to adjust exercise, sleep, and stress management by phase
- Sleep disruption during menstrual cycle not addressed
- Stress management strategies not phase-aware

**Current Gap**: Apps don't provide actionable lifestyle recommendations tied to cycle phases

## Health Monitoring & Early Detection Gaps

### Lack of Symptom Pattern Recognition

**Current Problem**
- Women don't track symptoms systematically
- Patterns go unnoticed until conditions become severe
- Early signs of PCOS, endometriosis, or hormonal imbalances missed

**Opportunity**
- Automated symptom tracking with pattern analysis
- Alerts for abnormal patterns (e.g., increasing pain, irregular cycles)
- Recommendations to seek medical help when patterns suggest disorders

### Missing Health Checkup Reminders

**Current Gap**
- No app reminds users to see gynecologist regularly
- Annual health screening not tracked
- Preventive care not encouraged

## Hydration, Nutrition & Self-Care Gaps

### Autonomous Health Checks Missing

**Current Problem**
- Women don't remember to hydrate adequately during periods
- Nutritional needs vary by phase but not tracked
- Self-care activities (stretching, meditation) not encouraged

**Opportunity for Saheli**
- **Smart Hydration Reminders**: "Drink 200ml water" - phase-aware frequency
- **Nutrition Alerts**: "Iron levels low - eat spinach, lentils today"
- **Self-Care Prompts**: "Try 5 min stretching" (menstrual), "Intense workout OK" (follicular)
- **Posture Reminders**: "Maintain good posture" - especially during heavy flow days
- **Rest Encouragement**: "Your body needs rest today - take it easy"

## Conclusion

The research clearly shows that **Indian women need more than just period tracking**—they need:
- **Health monitoring** with early detection of disorders
- **Personalized wellness guidance** tied to cycle phases
- **Ayurveda-informed recommendations** that resonate culturally
- **Mental health support** integrated with physical health
- **Autonomous reminders** for hydration, nutrition, and self-care
- **Education** to normalize menstruation and encourage medical care

Saheli has the opportunity to become the **most comprehensive, culturally-relevant period health app** for Indian women by addressing these gaps.
